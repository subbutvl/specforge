# Raw Idea

## Overview

Subscription Management is a lightweight personal and household workspace to keep all recurring subscriptions in one place.  
It focuses on visibility first: what subscriptions exist, what they cost, what their current status is, and whether they feel worth keeping.

## Initial Problem

People typically track subscriptions across scattered apps, emails, and payment sources.  
Because information is fragmented, it is hard to answer a simple question: "What all am I currently subscribed to?"

## Potential Users

- Individual consumers managing personal subscriptions
- Family/household members maintaining a shared subscription view

## Why This Idea Exists

- Subscription sprawl is common across apps, OTT, courses, software, and recurring bills
- Users need a single source of truth before they can optimize spending decisions
- A low-friction tracking workflow is more useful at this stage than automation-heavy tooling

## Initial Constraints

- Spec-first project: prioritize clarity and structure over implementation choices
- Lightweight workflow: avoid overengineering and enterprise process overhead
- v1-lite should center on unified visibility, not deep integrations

## Unknowns

- Alert/reminder strictness for renewals in v1-lite
- Review cadence policy for refreshing usage and worth signals
- Depth of categorization (fixed categories vs custom labels)
